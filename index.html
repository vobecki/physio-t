Dr. Amina Ochieng

I became a therapist because I know what it's like to feel like you're holding everything together on the outside while struggling on the inside. I've sat where you're sitting. And I know that asking for help is one of the hardest — and bravest — things you can do.

My approach is warm, practical, and honest. I won't just listen and nod — together we'll actually work through what's happening and figure out what needs to change. Sessions are in English and Swahili, and everything we talk about stays between us.

🔒 About Confidentiality

Everything discussed in sessions is completely private. I don't share your information with anyone — not your family, employer, or insurance provider — without your explicit written consent. The only legal exceptions are if there's immediate risk to your life or someone else's, which I'll always explain clearly upfront.

The first session is really just a conversation. I'll ask you some questions about what's been going on, what brought you here, and what you're hoping to get from therapy. You don't need to have it all figured out — most people don't. You can share as much or as little as you're comfortable with. By the end, we'll talk about whether working together feels like a good fit.
Individual sessions are KES 3,500 for 50 minutes. Couples sessions are KES 5,000. I offer a free 15-minute introductory call so you can ask questions and see if we're a good fit before committing. Payment is via M-Pesa or bank transfer before each session.
No. I don't share your name, your attendance, or anything you tell me with anyone else. If you pay out of pocket (not through insurance), there's no record with your employer or insurer. Many clients value the privacy of paying privately for exactly this reason. Your story is yours.
It depends on what you're working through. Some people feel significantly better after 6–8 sessions. Others find longer-term therapy more beneficial. I don't lock you into a set number — we review progress regularly and you decide when you feel ready to stop. There's no pressure to continue if therapy is no longer serving you.
